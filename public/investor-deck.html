The Problem

Vacant land is one
of the largest untapped
asset classes.

Millions of plots sit idle — unused, misused, or misunderstood. Not because there's no demand. Because the data is broken.

Land data is a maze of disconnected systems. Zoning, cadastre, building regulations, utilities — multiple sources, hundreds of pages of dense technical data. None of it connected. None of it machine-readable.

So the question "can I build here? Solar? Rural tourism? A data centre?" — has no fast answer. The opportunity is invisible.

1
High entropy data
Land data is unstructured, multimodal, fragmented and disconnected. PDFs, maps, registries, emails. Municipal plans locked in 500-page non-searchable documents. None of it works together.
2
AI cannot reason reliably
Without a structured data layer, AI models hallucinate on land queries. The models are ready. The data substrate is not.
3
Institutions cannot scale diligence
Solar developers, housing funds, ESG investors spend months manually checking plots — only to find disqualifying red flags an AI could spot in seconds.
4
Users fall back to manual checks
Without this layer, every buyer defaults to architects, lawyers, and consultants. The market hasn't changed in decades.
— a16z Big Ideas 2026
"The limiting factor for AI companies is now data entropy — the steady decay of freshness, structure, and truth inside the unstructured universe."
